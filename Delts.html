Delts

Dumbbell Shoulder Press

1. Sit on a bench with back support, holding a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder height.
2. Palms should be facing forward, and elbows should be bent at a 90-degree angle.
3. Push the dumbbells upward until your arms are fully extended, but not locked.
4. Slowly lower the dumbbells back to the starting position.

Standing Dumbbell Overhead Press

1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder height.
2. Palms should be facing forward, and elbows should be bent at a 90-degree angle.
3. Push the dumbbells upward until your arms are fully extended, but not locked.
4. Slowly lower the dumbbells back to the starting position.

Arnold Press

1. Sit on a bench with back support, holding a dumbbell in each hand at shoulder height.
2. Palms should be facing your body, and elbows bent at a 90-degree angle.
3. As you press the dumbbells upward, rotate your palms away from your body.
4. Continue to press until your arms are fully extended, with palms facing forward.
5. Reverse the motion to bring the dumbbells back down, rotating your palms back towards your body.

Lever Shoulder Press

1. Set the lever machine with the handles at shoulder height.
2. Sit with back flat against the pad and grip the handles.
3. Grip the handles with your arms extended and hands at shoulder height.
4. Press the handles forward until your arms are almost fully extended, but not locked.
5. Slowly bring the handles back to the starting position.

Two Arm Cable Front Raise

1. Set the cable machine with the handles at the lowest position.
2. Stand facing opposite the machine, holding the handle arms fully extended.
3. Keep the arms straight and raise them straight in front of you until they are at shoulder height.
4. Slowly lower the handles back down to the starting position.

Two Arm Dumbbell Front Raise

1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand.
2. Keep the arms straight and raise them straight in front of you until they are at shoulder height.
3. Slowly lower the dumbbells back down to the starting position.

Dumbbell Lateral Raise

1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand.
2. Maintain a slight bend in your elbows.
3. Raise both arms out to the sides until they are at shoulder level.
4. Slowly lower the dumbbells back down to your sides.

Single Hand Cable Lateral Raise

1. Set the cable machine with the handles at the lowest position.
2. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a handle in one hand.
3. Maintain a slight bend in your elbows.
4. Raise the arm out to the sides until it is at shoulder level.
5. Slowly lower the handles back down to your sides.

Seated Dumbbell Lateral Raise

1. Sit on a bench with back support, holding a dumbbell in each hand, arms down by your sides.
2. Maintain a slight bend in your elbows.
3. Raise both arms out to the sides until they are at shoulder level.
4. Slowly lower the dumbbells back down to your sides.

Rear Delt Machine Flys

1. Adjust the rear delt machine to your desired settings.
2. Sit on the machine with your chest against the pad and hold the handles with your arms extended.
3. Lower your arms down and slightly back until you feel a stretch in your rear delts.
4. Contract your rear deltoids to bring your arms back to the starting position.

Face Pull

1. Attach a rope handle to a high pulley on a cable machine and stand a few steps back.
2. Step forward and lean back slightly, arms extended.
3. Pull the handles towards your face, keeping your elbows high and wide.
4. Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the end of the movement.
5. Slowly return the handles to the starting position.

Inclined Dumbbell Reverse Fly

1. Set the bench to a comfortable incline, around 30 to 45 degrees.
2. Lie face-down on the inclined bench, holding a dumbbell in each hand, arms extended.
3. Begin with a slight bend in your elbows and the dumbbells directly below your shoulders.
4. Lift both arms out to the sides, squeezing your shoulder blades together at the top.
5. Stop when your arms are at shoulder level or slightly below.
6. Slowly lower the dumbbells back down to the starting position.

Dumbbell Rear Delt Row

1. Set up a bench and place a dumbbell on one side of it.
2. Stand on the side of the bench and place your knee and hand on the bench to support your body.
3. Pick up the dumbbell with your free hand, allowing it to hang directly below your shoulder.
4. Pull the dumbbell up keeping your elbow pointed upward and squeezing your shoulder blade.
5. Lower the dumbbell back down in a controlled manner and switch to the other side.

Bent over reverse cable fly

1. Set the cable machine with the handles at the lowest position.
2. Stand facing the machine, a few feet away, with a slight bend in your knees.
3. Bend at your hips to bring your torso parallel to the ground.
4. Grasp the handles with your arms extended and palms facing each other.
5. Pull the handles out to the sides, squeezing your shoulder blades together.
6. Pause at the end of the movement and slowly move back to the starting position.
